UncreditedUncredited is an interdisciplinary book and web project that explores the unfair attribution of achievements. At its core lies the question of why ideas, successes, and accomplishments are not always credited to the people who actually created them.The project highlights how perception is shaped by expectations, role models, and social conditioningčand how these influences can lead to systemic injustices. It is not about assigning simple blame, but about recognizing that judgments are rarely entirely neutral.Through theoretical content, historical examples, and an interactive website experience, Uncredited illustrates how bias, social structures, and stereotypical assumptions affect who we consider competent, whose voices we listen to, and whose work becomes visible.The goal of the project is to encourage self-reflection and raise awareness that injustice is not always obviousčit often begins where unnoticed expectations shape our perception.David Georg GroisChiara Rebecca KernRaphaela SchrittesserSarah FritzenwankerElena Hofmeister
